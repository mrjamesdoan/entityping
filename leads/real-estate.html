The Commercial Real Estate Prospecting Problem

Finding tenants and buyers before they find LoopNet is the competitive advantage that separates top producers.

Tenants Come to You After They Have Already Shopped

By the time a business owner calls you, they have already toured five spaces, seen pricing from three brokers, and formed opinions about what they want. You are negotiating against their expectations instead of guiding the process from the start.

Vacancy Sits While You Wait for Inbound

Listings on LoopNet and CoStar generate tire kickers and unqualified inquiries. Meanwhile, your vacancy costs the landlord thousands per month and your reputation suffers. You need a proactive pipeline of real prospects with real needs.

Cold Calling Existing Businesses Is a Dead End

Calling businesses that already have a lease is frustrating. They are locked in for 3-5 years and have no reason to move. You need people who are actively looking for space right now — not in two years when their lease expires.

Networking Events Yield Low-Quality Contacts

Chamber of commerce events and BNI groups produce connections, but rarely produce someone actively looking for space right now. You need prospects with an immediate, concrete need — not someone who might need something in 18 months.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can real estate agents use new business filing leads?
Every newly registered business is a potential tenant or buyer. Restaurants need retail space with kitchen infrastructure. Professional services firms need offices. E-commerce companies need warehouses. By contacting business owners within days of filing, you reach them before they start searching on LoopNet or calling other brokers.
Can I filter leads by industry to find businesses that need space?
Yes. EntityPing lets you filter by industry classification. Focus on business types that need physical locations — restaurants, retail stores, medical practices, fitness studios, salons, and professional services. Skip home-based businesses and online-only companies that do not need space.
What data is included with each lead?
Every lead includes the business owner's full name, business name, registered agent address, phone number, entity type (LLC, Corp, etc.), filing date, and industry classification. Growth and Pro plans also include email enrichment. The registered address often reveals whether they are still operating from home — a strong signal they need commercial space.
How do these leads compare to traditional CRE prospecting?
Traditional CRE prospecting relies on driving for dollars, cold calling existing businesses, or waiting for inbound inquiries. EntityPing gives you a daily list of brand-new businesses that have not leased space yet. You are reaching them at the earliest possible moment — before they have a broker, before they are on LoopNet, before they have signed a lease. The timing advantage is enormous.
