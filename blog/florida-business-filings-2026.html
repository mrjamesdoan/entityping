Florida Business Filings 2026: Trends, Stats, and Opportunities

Florida has consistently ranked as the top or second-largest state for new business formations in the United States. In 2026, the pace isn't slowing down. Here's what the data shows and how B2B companies can capitalize on it.

Florida's Business Formation Landscape

Florida has been on a sustained business formation boom since the early 2020s. The state saw approximately 800,000 new business filings in 2024, and preliminary data from the Florida Division of Corporations suggests that 2025 maintained a similar pace. Through Q1 2026, the trend shows no signs of slowing.

Several structural factors drive Florida's outsized share of new business creation:

  • No state income tax. Florida is one of nine states with no personal income tax, making it a natural destination for business owners looking to maximize take-home earnings. This advantage is especially compelling for LLC owners who pass business income through to their personal tax returns.
  • Population growth. Florida's population has grown by over 2 million since 2020, fueled by domestic migration from high-tax states like New York, California, and Illinois. More people means more demand for services and more entrepreneurs starting businesses to serve them.
  • Low regulatory burden. Florida consistently ranks among the top states for business-friendly regulation. The filing process itself is fast and inexpensive, with LLC formation costing as little as $125 through Sunbiz.org, the state's online filing portal.
  • Remote work migration. The post-pandemic shift to remote work brought a wave of workers and entrepreneurs to Florida, drawn by the climate, lower cost of living compared to coastal metros, and the lack of state income tax. Many formed businesses after relocating.
  • Tourism and hospitality. Florida's $100+ billion tourism industry creates a deep ecosystem of service businesses, from vacation rental management to restaurant supply companies, all of which need to register as legal entities.

Fastest-Growing Industries in Florida Filings

Not all industries are growing at the same rate. Based on EntityPing's analysis of Florida filing data over the past 12 months, here are the industries seeing the strongest growth in new formations:

1. Construction and Trades

Florida's sustained building boom, driven by population growth and storm-related rebuilding, has generated a massive increase in construction-related filings. General contractors, specialty trade contractors (electrical, plumbing, HVAC), and building material suppliers are forming new entities at a rate roughly 15% above the overall average. These businesses have immediate needs for liability insurance, bonding, equipment leasing, and material suppliers.

2. Real Estate and Property Management

With Florida's real estate market remaining active despite higher interest rates, new real estate agencies, property management companies, and vacation rental businesses continue to form at elevated rates. Many of these are single-agent or small-team operations that need CRM software, marketing support, and E&O insurance.

3. Health and Wellness Services

The aging demographics of Florida's population (the highest share of residents over 65 in the nation) drive steady demand for healthcare services. New filings in this category span medical practices, home health agencies, dental offices, mental health counseling practices, and wellness centers like med spas and chiropractic offices.

4. E-Commerce and Digital Services

Florida has become a hub for e-commerce businesses, Amazon FBA sellers, and digital marketing agencies. The combination of no income tax and a strong logistics infrastructure (major ports and multiple FedEx/UPS hubs) makes it ideal for online retail and fulfillment operations. These businesses need payment processing, shipping solutions, and digital marketing services.

5. Food Service and Hospitality

Restaurants, food trucks, catering companies, and hospitality-related businesses form a consistently large portion of Florida's new filings. These businesses have immediate needs for commercial insurance, POS systems, equipment financing, and food supplier relationships.

Regional Hotspots Within Florida

Florida's business formation activity is concentrated in several key metro areas, each with its own character and industry mix:

  • Miami-Dade County: The single largest county for new filings in the state, driven by international trade, finance, real estate, and a booming tech startup scene. Miami's appeal to Latin American entrepreneurs and the recent influx of crypto and fintech companies have pushed filing volumes even higher.
  • Hillsborough County (Tampa): A fast-growing metro with strong filing activity in healthcare, professional services, and construction. Tampa's relatively affordable cost of living compared to Miami makes it attractive to startups and small businesses.
  • Orange County (Orlando): Beyond the tourism and hospitality sector, Orlando has developed a growing technology and simulation industry cluster. New filings span tourism services, tech startups, and food service.
  • Broward County (Fort Lauderdale): Strong in marine industry, real estate, and professional services. The mix of established businesses and new formations creates a rich B2B ecosystem.
  • Palm Beach County: A hub for wealth management, luxury services, and healthcare. New business filings here tend to skew toward higher-value professional services.
  • Duval County (Jacksonville): The fastest-growing major metro in North Florida, with strong filing activity in logistics, healthcare, and financial services. Jacksonville's port and transportation infrastructure attract distribution and supply chain businesses.

How to Capitalize on Florida's Filing Boom

If you sell to small businesses and you aren't prospecting into Florida's new filings, you're leaving money on the table. Here's how to make the most of this market:

Target by Geography

Use geographic filters to focus on the counties and metro areas where your target industries are concentrated. If you sell commercial insurance in South Florida, filter for Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach counties. If you're a marketing agency targeting restaurants in Central Florida, filter for Orange and Osceola counties.

Target by Industry

EntityPing's industry classification lets you filter for the business types that match your offerings. A construction equipment supplier should target general contractors and specialty trade contractors. An accounting firm should target professional services, healthcare practices, and real estate agencies.

Move Fast

With over 2,000 new businesses filing every day in Florida, the volume is enormous. But so is the competition for these customers' attention. The businesses that reach out within 24-48 hours of the filing consistently see 3-4x higher response rates than those that wait a week or more.

Reference Florida-Specific Requirements

Florida has specific regulatory requirements that you can reference in your outreach to demonstrate expertise. For example, Florida requires workers' compensation insurance for businesses with four or more employees (just one for construction). Mentioning this in your outreach to a new construction LLC shows that you understand their situation.

Looking Ahead: What to Expect in 2026

Several factors suggest that Florida's business formation rate will remain strong through the rest of 2026 and beyond. Continued domestic migration, a growing population, infrastructure investment, and the state's tax-friendly environment all point to sustained filing volumes at or above current levels.

For B2B companies, the implication is clear: Florida is the single largest addressable market for new business leads in the United States. The businesses filing today are the customers you'll be serving tomorrow, but only if you reach them first.
